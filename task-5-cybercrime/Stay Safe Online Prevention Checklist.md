**Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist**



* UPI PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving: Remember that you never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks you to enter your PIN or scan a QR code to "credit" your account, it is a scam.



* Verify UPI IDs Before Paying: When paying at a local shop or to a new contact, always double-check the name that pops up on the screen before hitting 'Send' to ensure it matches the intended recipient.



* Use Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Enable 2FA on your Gmail, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Even if someone guesses your password, they won’t be able to log in without the secondary code on your phone.



* Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into your bank account or student portal while using free Wi-Fi at cafes, railway stations, or malls. Use your mobile data or a trusted VPN if necessary.



* Be Skeptical of "Urgent" Messages: If you get a WhatsApp message from a "friend" asking for an urgent loan or a stranger claiming your SIM card will be blocked, take a breath. Call the person directly to verify before taking any action.



* Secure Your Social Media Privacy: Set your profiles to 'Private' and regularly audit your 'Followers' or 'Friends' list. Avoid sharing your exact real-time location or photos of your ID cards/hall tickets.



* Check for "HTTPS" and Padlock Icons: Before entering credentials on any website—especially for scholarship applications or exam registrations—ensure the URL begins with https:// and shows a lock icon in the address bar.



* Regularly Update Your Software: Keep your phone’s OS and your apps updated. These updates often contain critical security patches that protect you against the latest malware and exploits.



* How to Report Cyber Crime
* If you or someone you know falls victim to a digital scam or online harassment, don't hesitate to use the official government channels:
* National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal: Visit cybercrime.gov.in to file a complaint online.
* Helpline Number: Dial 1930 immediately to report financial fraud and increase the chances of freezing the stolen funds.
