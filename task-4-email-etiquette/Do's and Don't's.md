

|Do's|Don't's|
|-|-|
|Audit your privacy settings regularly to ensure you know exactly who can see your personal posts and tagged photos.|Don't post in the heat of the moment. Avoid "venting" about professors, classmates, or employers while you are angry or frustrated.|
|Network with intent. Follow industry leaders, join professional groups, and use platforms like LinkedIn to connect with alumni.|Don't share sensitive personal info. Avoid posting your dorm room number, class schedule, or private phone number publicly.|
|Fact-check before sharing. Verify news or campus rumors through a second source to avoid spreading misinformation.|Don't engage with "trolls." Avoid getting into toxic digital arguments; it’s rarely productive and creates a permanent negative trail.|
|Showcase your projects. Use your platforms to highlight your portfolio, volunteer work, or extracurricular achievements.|Don't ignore the "Grandmother Rule." If you wouldn't want a grandparent or a future recruiter to see it, don't post it.|
|Give credit where it’s due. Always tag original creators or cite sources when sharing content that isn't yours.|Don't complain about your "grind." Constant public negativity about your workload can come across as unprofessional to future leads.|
|Curate a "Digital Portfolio" vibe. Even on casual platforms, occasionally share a post about a project you've completed or a workshop you attended to show you're active in your field.|Don't forget that "Private" isn't "Permanent." Always assume that anything you post—even in a private story or a DM—could be screenshotted and shared beyond your control.|
